Short Notes

Definition

[cite_start]

An expression $a^n$ means the variable **a** is raised to the power of **n**. [cite: 3] [cite_start]**n** is called the power, index, or exponent. [cite: 3]

    [cite_start]
  • $a^2 = a \times a$ [cite: 5]
    • [cite_start]
  • $a^3 = a \times a \times a$ [cite: 6]

Index Laws

These rules apply when the base (like 'a' or 'b') is the same.

    [cite_start]
  • Multiplication: $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$ (To multiply, add the indices) [cite: 10]
    • [cite_start]
  • Division: $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$ (To divide, subtract the indices) [cite: 11]
    • [cite_start]
  • Power of a Power: $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ (To raise a power to a power, multiply the indices) [cite: 12]
    • [cite_start]
  • Power of a Product: $(ab)^m = a^m b^m$ [cite: 13]
    • [cite_start]
  • Power of a Quotient: $(\frac{a}{b})^m = \frac{a^m}{b^m}$ [cite: 14]

Special Indices

    [cite_start]
  • Zero Index: $a^0 = 1$ (Any non-zero number to the power of 0 is 1) [cite: 20]
    • [cite_start]
  • Negative Index: $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$ (A negative index means "one over") [cite: 20]
    • [cite_start]
  • Fractional Index: $a^{\frac{1}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a}$ (e.g., $a^{\frac{1}{2}}$ is the square root of a) [cite: 21, 22]

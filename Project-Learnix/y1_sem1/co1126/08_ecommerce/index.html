Detailed Course Notes

1. E-Business vs. E-Commerce

    [cite_start]
  • E-Business (Electronic Business): The conducting of business on the Internet, which includes **not only buying and selling** but also servicing customers and collaborating with business partners [cite: 3088-3089].
  • E-Commerce (Electronic Commerce): The trading or facilitation of trading in products or services using computer networks like the Internet. [cite_start]This is primarily focused on the transaction (buying/selling)[cite: 3159].

2. EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)

[cite_start]

EDI is the **computer-to-computer exchange** of business documents in a **standard electronic format** between business partners[cite: 3094].

Manual Process vs. EDI Process

Manual Process EDI Process
Buyer generates PO, faxes or mails it. Buyer's internal system sends a digital PO.
Supplier manually enters the PO into their system. Supplier's system receives the PO instantly.
Supplier prints and mails an invoice. Supplier's system sends a digital Invoice.
Buyer manually enters the invoice for processing. Buyer's system receives invoice instantly.
[cite_start]

The key difference is that EDI documents flow straight to the application (Order Management System) with **no paper or people involved**[cite: 3099, 3114].

Key Components of EDI

    [cite_start]
  • Business Documents: The most common are purchase orders, invoices, and advance ship notices[cite: 3120].
  • Standard Format: Crucial because computers must be able to read and understand the documents. [cite_start]Examples include ANSI, EDIFACT, and ebXML[cite: 3124, 3127].
    • [cite_start]
  • Business Partners: The two different companies exchanging documents[cite: 3132].

3. Intranets, Extranets, and the Internet

    [cite_start]
  • Internet: The global network of interconnected computers, providing new business opportunities [cite: 3143-3144].
    • [cite_start]
  • Intranet: A private network that belongs to an organization, used to efficiently share work-related documents and facilitate collaboration among employees [cite: 3136-3137].
    • [cite_start]
  • Extranet: An intranet that allows authorized people *outside* the company (like suppliers, vendors, or partners) access to certain areas of the system[cite: 3140].
[cite_start]

Advantages of Extranets: More integrated supply chains, reduced costs (e.g., online manuals), and improved collaboration [cite: 3149-3151].

5. Types of E-Commerce

    [cite_start]
  • B2B (Business-to-Business): A business sells products/services to other businesses (e.g., Gates Rubber selling hoses to General Motors)[cite: 3194, 3202].
    • [cite_start]
  • B2C (Business-to-Consumer): A business sells products/services to individual customers (e.g., Amazon, Netflix)[cite: 3196, 3218].
    • [cite_start]
  • C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer): Consumers sell directly to other consumers (e.g., OLX, Quickr)[cite: 3199].
    • [cite_start]
  • C2B (Consumer-to-Business): Consumers sell to businesses[cite: 3200].

B2B Materials

    [cite_start]
  • MRO (Maintenance, Repair, Operations): Indirect materials that do not relate directly to production (e.g., office supplies, repair parts) [cite: 3208-3210].
    • [cite_start]
  • Direct Materials: Materials used in production (e.g., rubber for tires) [cite: 3211-3212].

B2C Products & Payments

    [cite_start]
  • Product Types: Convenience, Specialty, Commodity-like, Digital [cite: 3230-3237].
    • [cite_start]
  • Payment Systems: Financial Cybermediary, Electronic Check, EBPP (Electronic Bill Presentment and Payment), Smart Card [cite: 3240-3242].

6. E-Government

[cite_start]

The application of ICT (Information and Communication Technology) for delivering government services[cite: 3244].

  • G2C (Government-to-Customer)
  • G2B (Government-to-Business)
  • G2G (Government-to-Government)
[cite_start]

Advantages: Simplifies information accumulation, empowers citizens, and increases transparency, which helps eliminate corruption [cite: 3246-3249].

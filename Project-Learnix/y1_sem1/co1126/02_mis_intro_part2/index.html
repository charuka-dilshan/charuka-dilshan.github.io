Detailed Course Notes

1. The Importance of MIS

In today's complex business world, a properly planned and maintained MIS is critical for survival and growth. [cite_start]It provides **timely, reliable, and useful information** to help management make speedy and rational decisions[cite: 2195].

Two Main Challenges for Managers:

  1. Liberalization and Globalization: Organizations must compete globally, not just locally. [cite_start]A manager must make quick decisions, or business will be taken by competitors [cite: 2197-2198].
  2. The Information Age: Information doubles every two to three years. [cite_start]A manager must process large, voluminous data to avoid making costly wrong decisions [cite: 2199-2200].
[cite_start]

MIS is considered the "nerve center" of an organization[cite: 2202]. [cite_start]It assists decision-makers at various stages and provides a competitive edge, allowing even small organizations to offset the economies of scale enjoyed by bigger competitors[cite: 2206].

2. Computers and MIS

[cite_start]

Computers and information technology are used to automate the critical functions of an MIS[cite: 2212].

    [cite_start]
  • Processing Speed & Accuracy: Computers can process certain types of business data (like accounting) quicker and more accurately than employees [cite: 2216-2217].
  • Access Control & Security: Using computers allows a company to restrict employee access to sensitive data. [cite_start]Most systems require usernames and passwords, which prevents individuals from filtering or altering information before managers review it [cite: 2218-2220].

3. Organizational Structure

MIS supports all functional areas and levels of an organization. [cite_start]A typical structure includes [cite: 2225-2241]:

  • Top Level: President
  • Management Level: R&D Manager, Finance Manager, Marketing Manager, Manufacturing Manager.
  • Operational Level:
    • R&D: Design, Development, Testing
    • Finance: Accounting, Purchasing
    • Marketing: Sales, Customer Service
    • Manufacturing: Line Supervisors, Production Teams, Distribution

1. The Digital Economy

[cite_start]

The **digital economy** (also called the Internet economy, new economy, or Web economy) is an economy based on digital technologies, including communication networks (Internet, intranets, extranets), computers, and software [cite: 3502-3503].

[cite_start]

This provides a global platform for interaction, communication, and collaboration[cite: 3504].

Components of the Digital Economy:

    [cite_start]
  • Digitizable Products: Products that can be converted to a digital format, such as books, movies, magazines, music, and software [cite: 3507-3508].
    • [cite_start]
  • Digital Transactions: Consumers and firms conducting financial transactions digitally[cite: 3509].
    • [cite_start]
  • Embedded Tech: Physical goods that contain embedded computer chips and connectivity (e.g., smart appliances)[cite: 3510].

2. Information Flow in Organizations

[cite_start]

Information flows in four distinct directions within a business [cite: 3520-3521]:

  • Upward Flow: Describes the current state of the organization. [cite_start]Starts as detailed daily transactions (at the lower level) and is passed upward, becoming summarized for upper management [cite: 3522-3523].
  • Downward Flow: Strategies, goals, and directives originate at a higher level and are passed to lower levels. [cite_start]Upper levels develop strategies, middle levels convert them to tactics, and lower levels handle operational details [cite: 3524-3525].
    • [cite_start]
  • Horizontal Flow: Communication between different units or departments at the same level (e.g., sales informing production)[cite: 3526].
    • [cite_start]
  • Inward/Outward Flow: Communication with external entities such as customers, suppliers, distributors, and partners [cite: 3528-3529].

3. People: The Knowledge Worker

People are a key resource. Modern employees must be both:

  • Technology-Literate: Knows how and when to apply technology. [cite_start]This includes what tech to buy, how to use software, and what infrastructure is needed [cite: 3532-3533].
    • [cite_start]
  • Information-Literate: Can define what information is needed, knows how/where to get it, understands it, and can act on it to help the organization [cite: 3541-3543].
[cite_start]

Ethics are the principles and standards that guide our behavior toward other people[cite: 3545].

Memory vs. Storage [cite: 3617]

It is crucial to understand this difference:

    [cite_start]
  • RAM (Memory): Is **Volatile** (loses data when power is off) and provides **Fast Access** [cite: 3620-3621].
    • [cite_start]
  • Hard Drive (Storage): Is **Non-volatile** (keeps data when power is off), has **Large Capacity**, and **Slower Access** [cite: 3625-3627].

5. Information Technology (IT): Software

    [cite_start]
  • Application Software: Designed to perform specific tasks for the user (e.g., Word processor, Spreadsheet, Web browser)[cite: 3650].
    • [cite_start]
  • System Software: Provides services to other software[cite: 3672].
      [cite_start]
    • Operating System (OS): Manages all hardware and software (e.g., Windows, MacOS, Linux)[cite: 3673].
    • [cite_start]
  • Utility Software: Designed to help analyze, configure, optimize, or maintain a computer (e.g., Antivirus, Disk Formatting) [cite: 3687-3689].
